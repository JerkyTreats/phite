Genetic Insights: Diabetes Risk Profile

Overview

Based on your genetic profile, you carry several markers associated with an increased risk for insulin resistance and impaired insulin secretion. These markers affect how efficiently your body responds to and manages blood glucose levels. While your genes do not guarantee the development of diabetes, they do indicate a heightened baseline susceptibility that can be influenced by diet, lifestyle, and nutritional support.

Key Takeaways

  • Elevated risk for impaired insulin secretion due to homozygous variants in KCNJ11 and HHEX.
  • Evidence of insulin resistance related to the IRS1 variant.
  • One protective variant found in SLC30A8, related to zinc-dependent insulin processing.
  • These genetic factors can be influenced positively through proactive lifestyle management.

Mitigation Strategies

Exercise

Nutrition

  • Focus on a low-glycemic, high-fiber diet including whole grains, legumes, vegetables, and healthy fats.
  • Reduce intake of refined sugars and processed carbohydrates that can spike blood glucose.

Supplementation

  • Ensure adequate zinc intake to support insulin production (especially relevant due to SLC30A8 variant).
  • Consider magnesium for improved insulin sensitivity and glucose metabolism.
  • Monitor fasting glucose and HbA1c levels regularly to guide intervention efficacy.
